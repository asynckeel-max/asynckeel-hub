# 📣 AsyncKeel Social Media Strategy

## Platform Priorities

### 1) Twitter/X (Highest Priority)
**Why**: Fast feedback loops, strong indie-hacker and dev-tool audience, ideal for build-in-public momentum.

### 2) LinkedIn
**Why**: Better reach for startup teams, technical founders, and decision-makers considering paid tiers.

### 3) Reddit
**Why**: High-signal discussions in niche communities when content is practical and non-promotional.

### 4) Hacker News
**Why**: Strong discovery channel for developer products through Show HN and technical transparency.

### 5) Dev.to
**Why**: Long-form technical distribution and SEO-friendly developer audience.

## Tone and Content by Platform

### Twitter/X
- **Tone**: concise, transparent, builder-focused
- **Content**: launch updates, feature highlights, snippets, short lessons

### LinkedIn
- **Tone**: practical and professional, still approachable
- **Content**: startup/backend insights, milestones, case-style posts

### Reddit
- **Tone**: helpful, no hype, discussion-first
- **Content**: guides, postmortems, “what worked/failed” learnings

### Hacker News
- **Tone**: technical and direct
- **Content**: architecture choices, benchmarks, launch write-ups

### Dev.to
- **Tone**: educational and step-by-step
- **Content**: tutorials, implementation breakdowns, best practices

## Community Engagement Tactics

### Reddit (r/Python, r/webdev)
- Lead with educational content, not product links.
- Share implementation lessons and ask specific technical questions.
- Follow each subreddit’s self-promo rules strictly.

### Hacker News (Show HN)
- Post only when the product demo/repo is genuinely usable.
- Include technical decisions, trade-offs, and what feedback is needed.
- Stay active in comments for at least 24 hours post-launch.

### General Engagement Rules
- Reply within 24 hours to genuine questions.
- Track recurring objections and turn them into content.
- Celebrate user wins and community contributions.

## Success Metrics

### Awareness
- Impressions per platform
- Follower growth rate
- Mention/share velocity

### Engagement
- Engagement rate (likes + comments + shares / impressions)
- Comment quality (technical depth and intent)
- Click-through rate to docs/GitHub

### Conversion
- GitHub stars from social traffic
- Waitlist or sign-up conversion (if active)
- Pro tier interest signals (DMs, demo requests, pricing page clicks)
